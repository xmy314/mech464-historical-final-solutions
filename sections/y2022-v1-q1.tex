\begin{solutionbox}[Q1-3]
    $Qs = Is$
    $(Q-I)s = 0$

    The output space of Q-I is necessarily of dimension 2 since component perpendicular to s gets canceled out.

    Columns of Q-I are just Q-I applied to basis vectors which also have to lie in the 2d space.

    Take two columns of Q-I, normalize them and call them u and $v_t$. Subscript t for temporary.

    Normalize $u \times v_t$ and call it s.

    Normalize $s \times u$ and call it v.

    Now u v s form a right hand orthonormal basis.

    $Q u \cdot u$ is then the cosine of the angle.
    $Q u \cdot v$ is then the sine of the angle.

    Use atan2 and the angle is found.

    For the other solution, negate both s and theta works too.
\end{solutionbox}

    \end{align*}
